INFP

( Introverted, Intuitive, Feeling, Prospecting )

Mediator

About

  • INFP is a thoughtful, compassionate, and introspective individual who values authenticity and deep personal meaning.
  • Guided by strong inner values, INFPs are driven to make the world a better place and often feel a deep sense of purpose.
  • They are imaginative and open-minded, often drawn to creative expression and meaningful connections with others.
  • Though they may seem quiet or reserved, INFPs have rich inner worlds and are deeply passionate about the people and causes they care about.

Strength

  • INFPs care deeply for others and believe it is their duty to make a positive impact on the lives of other people in any way they can.
  • There is no denying that the deeply reflective nature of INFPs allows them to transcend the boundaries of imagination that so often prevent us from discovering new solutions to old problems.
  • They are enthusiastic advocates of cooperation and believe that no difficulty is insurmountable when people work together for a common cause.
  • INFPs have a unique ability to mold and shape their surrounding environment in ways that promote self-improvement and transcendent achievement for all.

Weaknesses

  • INFPs can end up retreating into lethargy and depression when they discover their idealism isnâ€™t always shared or respected by others, and their incredible talents can go completely to waste when they become too discouraged to continue.
  • The intensity of their feelings can occasionally leave INFPs unwilling to make compromises even when doing so might be necessary to get something accomplished.
  • INFPs have a tendency to neglect or suppress their own needs if they believe it is necessary to keep the peace or make others happy.

Relationship

  • In relationships, the INFP is nurturing, empathic, and loyal. Healers select their friends and partners carefully, looking for a strong bond and congruent values.
  • They support their partners' individuality, and encourage them to explore their interests and ideas.
  • They can be very sensitive, but often keep negative reactions to themselves because they are reluctant to engage in confrontation.
  • Close and harmonious relationships are important to INFPs, although they also need a lot of independent time to think and reflect. They often want plenty of freedom to express themselves and pursue greater self-awareness.

Career

  • INFPs are not particularly driven by money or status, preferring work that aligns with their personal values and allows them to help others.
  • INFPs are typically motivated by vision and inspiration, and want to engage with projects and causes that feel important to them.
  • They enjoy the process of creative problem-solving, and want to understand complex issues. They appreciate innovation, and want to come up with original ideas to improve circumstances for people.
  • When they work with other people, it's important to the INFP that they be cooperative, supportive, and flexible, and that they have similar passion for their ideals.
