ENFJ

( Extraverted, Intuitive, Feeling, Judging )

Protagonist

About

  • ENFJs are idealist organizers, driven to implement their vision of what is best for humanity. They often act as catalysts for human growth .
  • They are focused on values and vision, and are passionate about the possibilities for people.
  • ENFJs are typically energetic and driven, and often have a lot on their plates. They are tuned into the needs of others and acutely aware of human suffering.
  • The ENFJ is ambitious, but their ambition is not self-serving: rather, they feel personally responsible for making the world a better place.

Strength

  • ENFJs not only work to promote harmony and morale while deflecting potential conflicts, they are also deft in persuading and motivating others to get moving in the same direction.
  • As comfortable in large crowds as small groups, they like to be the center of attention and thrive on the energy of good conversation, especially with people who are as passionate as they are.
  • ENFJs are known for their powers of persuasion and are generally effective in getting people to do what they want them to do. These personalities are likeable and, as a result, others are generally compelled to listen to and follow them.
  • Teaching and mentoring come as easily to them as breathing and talking, making ENFJs particularly skilled in endeavors that allow them to lead, train or educate.

Weaknesses

  • Though they thrive on being a little overwhelmed, ENFJs can easily become over-committed because they are genuinely excited about life and all of the opportunities they see before them.
  • They may also be seen as unfair since they are likely to prioritize the needs of the individual above the whole, and to look more readily to the exceptions than the rules.
  • They may not be properly in touch with their own needs and can struggle, at times, to express them if they fear that doing so may create conflict or disunity.

Relationship

  • In relationships, the ENFJ is helpful and enthusiastically supportive. They are motivated to understand their partners and are sensitive to the emotions of their mates.
  • ENFJs are very sensitive to criticism and can become highly emotional and even punishing when their feelings are hurt.
  • ENFJ partners want harmony above all else, sometimes at the expense of their own needs.
  • The ideal mate for an ENFJ appreciates their compassion, support, and dedication to helping others, and makes an effort to understand the ENFJ's feelings and values.

Career

  • ENFJs are enthusiastic problem-solvers, especially when they can put their strong intuition about people to good use.
  • ENFJs are often attracted to leadership roles; they naturally organize people to take advantage of their unique talents.
  • The ideal work environment for an ENFJ is forward-thinking and people-centered, with a clear humanitarian mission and an emphasis on constructive action.
  • The ideal job for an ENFJ allows them to develop and implement ideas that improve the circumstances and well-being of other people.
